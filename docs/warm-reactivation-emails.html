Warm reactivation · Week 1 sprint

Three Emails to Send This Weekend

Three audience-specific templates for warm reactivation. Send one at a time, personalized — never broadcast. Goal: 100 sent by end of week 1, ~15 replies, 3–5 introductions, 1–3 real project leads.

The rule that makes this work

Specificity is the whole game. A two-sentence personal opener that proves you remember them moves the response rate from 8% to 40%. Don't skip it. If you can't think of one specific thing about the person, skip the person.

How to use these templates

  1. Pick the right variant for the relationship (see the rubric below).
  2. Write a fresh opener. One or two sentences that prove you remember them. Examples in the "Personalization bank" section.
  3. Paste the template body after your opener. Don't rewrite the two-question structure — it's doing the work.
  4. Send from your personal Gmail (or LinkedIn DM for the dormant connections). Don't send from noreply@ascendsystems.ai — these are personal, not transactional.
  5. Log each send in the Ascend CRM. Tag source=warm-reactivation, warmth=warm, set last-touch to today.
  6. Space sends across the weekend — 15–20 per session, not 100 in one sitting. Replies will start landing within hours; you want to be present to respond.

Variant A — Past Client & Collaborator

Variant A · Past Client

Best for: anyone who paid you, anyone you shipped work alongside.

Subject lines (pick one)

  • Quick update on what I'm working on
  • Following up after [project name]
  • Quick check-in + a small ask

Body

Hey [Name] —

[1–2 sentence personal opener — see Personalization bank
below. Something specific about them, their business, or the
last time you talked.]

Wanted to share what I've been up to. I've turned the
consulting work into a focused practice — Ascend Systems —
helping Charlotte SMBs with custom CRMs, AI integrations,
and fractional CTO work. Same scope of build we did
on [reference past project / shared work], just more
of it, more focused.

Recent ones: shipped the EV charging dashboard for the
City of Charlotte, built a multi-tenant ops platform for
Stancil Services.

Two questions for you:

1. Anything on your "I should fix this" list that's been
   sitting too long? Happy to look at it free, no commitment
   — I owe you that for the work we did together.

2. Do you know one Charlotte business owner I should meet?
   Anyone running a $1M–$50M services or trades company
   who's still on spreadsheets is exactly the right fit.

Either way, good to hear what you're working on these days.

— Brian

Why this version

Past clients already know the work. The lift is mentioning the past project specifically (proves you remember the engagement, not just the name), offering free help as relationship maintenance (low pressure), and asking for one specific intro.

Variant B — Friend in Business

Variant B · Friend

Best for: personal friends, family who run businesses, people you'd grab coffee with anyway.

Subject lines (pick one)

  • What I'm focused on now
  • Quick favor + an update
  • Need your brain for 2 minutes

Body

Hey [Name] —

[1–2 sentence personal opener — kids, last hangout,
recent shared thing, something they posted about. Should
not feel like a pitch is coming.]

Quick update since I haven't told you what I'm spending
my work hours on. I've started a tech consultancy out
here in Charlotte — Ascend Systems. Custom software,
CRMs, AI integrations, fractional CTO work for small
and mid-sized companies. Built the EV charging dashboard
for the City of Charlotte last year and a multi-tenant ops
platform for Stancil Services this year, both shipped and
live.

Need your brain for two things:

1. Anyone in your network running a Charlotte business in
   the $1M–$50M range who's drowning in manual work or
   thinking about hiring a tech lead? Would love an intro
   if it makes sense.

2. If anything in your own business has been on the "we
   should automate this" list too long, I'll take a look
   for free. No sales pitch — call it a friendship favor.

Drinks soon — I owe you one. Tell [spouse/kid/recent shared
thing] I said hi.

— Brian

Why this version

Personal tone front and back. The ask is framed as "need your brain" not "want to sell to you." The offer is "friendship favor" — removes the awkward "selling to a friend" feeling on both sides. The close keeps it social with a real follow-up commitment.

Personalization bank — opener examples

Use real material. If nothing comes to mind, skip the person rather than fake it. Generic openers ("hope you're well!") kill the email.

Variant A openers (Past Client / Collaborator)

  • "Been thinking about [specific thing from the project] lately — it's funny how the [feature/workflow] we shipped is now the thing I show in every demo."
  • "Saw [their company / their name] in [LinkedIn post / press mention / industry list] last week — congrats on [specific milestone]."
  • "It's been about [N months/years] since we wrapped [project name] — wanted to circle back."
  • "Wrapped a project last week that reminded me of the [specific challenge] we hit on [past project] — figured I'd send a note."

Variant B openers (Friend in Business)

  • "How's [spouse / kid / dog / recent thing they mentioned]?"
  • "Still owe you a [coffee / round / dinner] from [event / context]."
  • "Saw your post about [thing] — [genuine reaction]."
  • "[Mutual friend] mentioned you've been heads-down on [thing] — how's that going?"

Variant C openers (Dormant LinkedIn)

  • "We connected back when [context — where, what event, mutual connection]. Time flies."
  • "Saw your recent post about [specific topic] — [genuine one-line reaction]."
  • "Noticed you moved from [old company] to [new company] — congrats. Hope the new chapter is going well."
  • "Came across your name again on [Charlotte Inno / CBJ / shared LinkedIn post] — figured this was the nudge to actually reach out."

Reply playbook — what to do when they respond

Reply typeYour move
"Yes, I have a project." Reply with two calendar slots in the next 5 business days. 20-min call. Don't ask them to fill out a form. discovery-call in CRM, promote to hot.
"Yes, let me intro you to X." Reply: "Awesome — happy with email intros or LinkedIn. Here's a one-paragraph blurb you can forward if helpful: [3-sentence summary of what you do + recent proof + ICP]." Make the intro free for them.
"Tell me more first." One short paragraph + a link to ascendsystems.ai/portfolio + offer a 20-min call. Don't send a pitch deck.
"Not right now, but I'll keep you in mind." Reply: "Got it. Mind if I check in once a quarter?" Add to a warm-quarterly list. Genuinely follow up.
"Send me your pricing." Don't. Reply: "Pricing depends on scope — let me ask 2 questions on a 20-min call so I can give you a real number." Drop calendar link.
No reply after 14 days. One soft bump: "Hey [Name], no pressure — just bumping this in case it got buried. If now's not the moment, no worries at all." Then close the loop.

Tracking in the Ascend CRM

Every send gets logged. Don't trust your inbox to remember. Open ascendsystems.ai/admin/projects and create a lead per recipient with these tags:

  • source = warm-reactivation
  • warmth = warm (or hot after they reply positively)
  • variant = a / b / c (so we can measure reply rates by audience type)
  • last-touch-date = YYYY-MM-DD
  • next-action = "wait for reply" / "book call" / "follow up [date]"

One last reminder before you start

Replies will start landing within an hour of the first send. Check your inbox — but don't open the floodgates by responding to one and starting a new send block in the same window. Batch the sends, then batch the replies. Otherwise you'll burn the whole weekend and only get through 30 emails.
