Ascend Systems — Design Audit

A code-level review of the site that is about to receive cold outreach traffic. Five dimensions — accessibility, performance, theming, responsive design, and anti-patterns — scored P0–P3, with the smallest defensible fix for each issue.

TL;DR · overall grade

Grade: C−

Score: 11 / 20. The site looks polished at first glance — strong typography, intentional charcoal/orange brand, real case studies on /portfolio. But it carries three loaded weapons aimed at its own credibility: fabricated hero stats, fabricated testimonials, and a fabricated client marquee. Any prospect who clicks one Google search ("Meridian Freight COO Priya Shankar") finds nothing, and the whole site loses trust in a heartbeat.

Do not send outreach traffic to this site until the three P0 trust issues are fixed. Everything else (perf, a11y, responsive) is fixable in a normal week.

Two competing design-token systems src/styles/globals.css L6–35 vs src/styles/ascend-design-system.css L6–107 P1 Theming The site has two parallel sources of truth for the brand. globals.css defines --color-charcoal: #1E2A32 and --color-orange: #D4632C. ascend-design-system.css defines --color-primary: #1C1C1E and --color-accent: #C45A2C. Same brand, different hex. The doc style guide (this file's stylesheet) uses a third set. Components pick whichever variable they happen to import. Pick one canonical set (recommend ascend-design-system.css — the values match the doc style guide and the printed brand kit). No prefers-reduced-motion guards Multiple — HeroSection.tsx framer-motion entries, ascend-design-system.css L345–360 marquee, globals.css L37–45 keyframes, ScrollReveal P1 A11y · WCAG 2.3.3 Every entry animates with opacity 0 → 1, y 20 → 0, the client logo strip marquees forever, and scroll-reveal fires on every section. For users with vestibular sensitivity (≈ 1 in 3 over 40), the homepage is a physical irritant. Also: the marquee runs an infinite animation on the main thread. Wrap motion blocks in @media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference) in CSS, and use useReducedMotion() from framer-motion in the hero. Pause the marquee on reduced-motion users entirely.
Three-column grids never collapse below 900 px src/components/CaseStudiesSection.tsx L84–90, src/components/TestimonialsSection.tsx L64–69 P1 Responsive Both sections use inline style with gridTemplateColumns: 'repeat(3, 1fr)' with no media query. Inline styles can't be overridden by the .section { padding } @media block. On a 768 px tablet, three case-study cards squeeze into ~230 px each and the stat numbers wrap. Below 640 px the cards become unreadable. Move both grids to Tailwind utilities: className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-3 gap-6". What's actually good

The audit grade is C−, but most of that score is sitting on three fixable trust issues and one bundle-splitting fix. Underneath, the site has real foundations worth keeping.

  • Real portfolio. /portfolio and the per-project case studies are honest, specific, and well-structured. The data model in src/data/portfolio.ts with status flags (live / building / on-hold) is the kind of detail that signals "operator-built, not marketing-built."
  • The cost calculator is a strong lead magnet. The form copy ("Four numbers, one honest answer", "no sales pitch", "we do not sell or share your email"), the on-page result, and the email follow-up are well thought out. The math model with build_band / payback ranges is more rigorous than 90% of consultancy calculators. Fix the form a11y and ship it.
  • Typography discipline. Inter + JetBrains Mono pairing, balanced text-wrap, tight letter-spacing on display sizes, eyebrow micro-headers in mono — the type system is the strongest piece of the design.
  • Charcoal & burnt-orange brand is distinctive. In a category drowning in cobalt-blue gradients, the warm-dark direction reads grown-up and Charlotte-specific. Worth defending.
  • Mostly semantic HTML. Headings are real headings, the form uses real <label> + htmlFor, the testimonial uses <figure>/<figcaption>. The bones are right; the ARIA wiring is what's missing.
  • The Contact and Cost-Calculator copy is in Brian's actual voice. "Tell us what you are working on. We will get back to you within one business day with an honest take on timeline, cost, and whether we can help." That's the voice that should be on the home page too — not the fabricated-VP voice in the testimonials.

Patterns & systemic issues

  1. Inline styles vs. design system. Two parallel approaches coexist: a well-built design system (ascend-design-system.css + Tailwind @theme) and inline style objects with hard-coded pixels. The home-page sections all use inline styles; the cost calculator uses utilities. Pick one. The utility/token path is the right one — the system already exists.
  2. Trust signals invented to fill the page. Hero stats, testimonials, and client marquee all fabricate proof Brian doesn't have yet. The site has real proof (City of Charlotte EV Dashboard is a serious win) but is hiding it behind invented proof. Inversion needed: lead with the two real engagements, drop the stats, drop the names that aren't real.
  3. No code-splitting strategy. Every chunk lives in one bundle — admin, portal, public, calculator, even the Stripe-ish proposal-sign flow. Vite's rollupOptions.output.manualChunks isn't configured. Adding React.lazy per route group is the entire fix.
  4. Animation is on by default everywhere. Framer-motion entry on every component, scroll-reveal class on every section, infinite marquee, pulse-glow keyframes. No prefers-reduced-motion escape hatch. Strip the animations to the 2–3 that earn their weight.

Recommended next commands

In priority order, mapped to the relevant Claude skill:

  1. P0 /clarify — rewrite or remove the three trust-signal blocks (hero stats, testimonials, client marquee) with content that matches Brian's actual track record. Honest beats impressive on a site that already has real case studies.
  2. P0 /optimize — code-split the admin/portal routes with React.lazy, convert hero JPEG to preloaded WebP, compress logo PNG to SVG. Target: ≤ 200 KB initial JS, ≤ 100 KB initial image weight.
  3. P1 /harden — wire up aria-invalid/aria-describedby on the calculator and contact forms, add fieldset/legend on the radio group, add skip-to-content, add aria-expanded on the mobile nav.
  4. P1 /adapt — fix the inline three-column grids on case studies and testimonials so they collapse below 900 px and 640 px. Audit any other inline repeat(3, 1fr) grids.
  5. P1 /quieter — gate every motion effect behind prefers-reduced-motion, prune the framer-motion entries down to the hero + one section-level reveal, replace the infinite marquee with a static row.
  6. P2 /distill — consolidate the two token systems into one (recommend the ascend-design-system.css set), migrate the home-page sections off inline styles onto the utility classes that already exist.
  7. P2 /polish — final pass on the small stuff: dead anchor links in the hero, hard-coded "7 projects" string, oversized footer logo, decorative code snippets read by screen readers.
