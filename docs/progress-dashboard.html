2 · SEO for AI Tools

Getting cited by Claude, ChatGPT, Grok, and Perplexity. Crawlability is done; visibility isn't.

The honest picture: this isn't a separate tech checklist. The crawlers are already allowed and the schema is better than most. What actually drives citation is Bing indexing and third-party mentions — neither is done.
  • AI crawlers explicitly allowed in robots.txtGPTBot, ClaudeBot, anthropic-ai, PerplexityBot, Bingbot.
  • llms.txt publishedLive at /llms.txt. Cheap to maintain — but unproven, no major provider has committed to reading it. Don't expect much.
  • Structured data for clean extractionFAQPage and Service schema give models labelled Q&A pairs rather than prose to guess at.
  • Specific, citable facts in the content"2–3 hours → 15 minutes", "208 stations across 46 locations", "683 organic visitors/month". Models cite specifics, not adjectives.
  • Set up Bing Webmaster ToolsHighest-leverage item on this page. ChatGPT and Perplexity lean heavily on Bing's index. If you're only in Google, you're invisible to a large share of AI search.
  • Third-party mentions and directory listingsModels cite roundups, directories, and review sites more than vendor homepages. "Best Charlotte software developers" listicles matter more than our own copy.
  • Baseline citation testAsk Claude / ChatGPT / Grok "who builds custom software in Charlotte NC" and record the answer. Repeat monthly — that's the only real measurement available.
  • Question-shaped content for extractionH2 = literal user query, followed by a 40–60 word direct answer. Works for both featured snippets and AI citation.
