Methodology

Sources mined

  • Inc. 5000 (2024) — Charlotte-region honorees, focusing on companies likely in the $5M–$50M revenue band based on the growth rates published
  • Charlotte Business Journal Fast 50 (2024 and 2025 editions, public references only — full ranked list is paywalled)
  • Best Pick Reports / Expertise.com / Mynd / Stessa aggregator reviews for property management
  • Charlotte-area trade publications — Consumer Affairs, Charlotte Observer "best of" picks, BBB profiles
  • Industry-specific roundups — Inven, Built In Charlotte, Clutch.co, Best Lawyers, ClearlyRated
  • Direct company websites — verified each link before adding to the table

Qualification rules applied

  • Geography: HQ in Mecklenburg, Union, Cabarrus, Iredell, Lincoln, Gaston, or York SC. Regional/national companies with a strong Charlotte office included only if there's a clear local decision-maker.
  • Size: Targeted $1M–$50M revenue or ~10–250 employees. Companies clearly above this band excluded (see Exclusions).
  • Decision-maker access: Strong preference for founder/owner-led firms. PE-backed rollups deprioritized to P3 because procurement is structured.
  • Honest uncertainty: Where revenue/HC isn't public, marked as "est." with the basis. If unknown, said so.

Priority scoring

  • P1 — Inc. 5000 / Fast 50 honoree (growth = pain), founder still in seat, mid-size enough to need real systems but small enough to talk directly
  • P2 — Fits ICP cleanly but no public growth signal, OR company is the right size but the decision-maker is layered behind operations leadership
  • P3 — Fits ICP at the edges (slightly oversize, PE-backed, or industry-adjacent). Worth keeping in the file but lower-yield for cold outreach.

This is a seed list, not the final list. Treat the 62 names above as the spine of a CRM file you'll grow to 500+ over the next 90 days. Here's how:

  1. Verify decision-makers via LinkedIn (1 evening). Most rows say "(Owner — verify)" — that's your first job. For each P1, find the founder/CEO/Operations VP on LinkedIn and add their name, title, and direct connection signal (mutual connections, alma mater, recent posts).
  2. Subscribe to Charlotte Business Journal ($150/yr). The Book of Lists alone is worth it. The Fast 50 and Largest Private Companies lists are paywalled but they're the highest-signal sources for our ICP. Download every list as a CSV.
  3. Mine each Inc 5000 / Fast 50 honoree's "About Us" + LinkedIn. Every growing Charlotte company has 3–5 vendor/supplier/partner companies of similar size on their site. That doubles the list overnight.
  4. Add chambers as a manual harvest source. The Lake Norman Chamber alone has 900+ investors. Even a 5% ICP hit rate = 45 more names. Charlotte Regional Business Alliance is bigger.
  5. Use Built In Charlotte + Glassdoor for size validation. Headcount is publicly listed and gives a defensible revenue estimate ($150K–$250K rev per employee for service businesses; $300K+ for product/SaaS).
  6. Spin up a tagging system in the CRM. Don't reach out cold to all 62. Bucket them: "warm intro available," "founder podcast guest," "shared connection," "needs research." Start with the warm bucket.
  7. Pitch by signal, not by industry. The Inc 5000 honorees all share the same pain: 3 years of 100%+ growth = duct-taped systems. Lead with that, not with "I do CRMs."
