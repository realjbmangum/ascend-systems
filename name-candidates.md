## Evaluation Criteria

Each name is scored on:
- **.com likelihood** — How likely the .com domain is available (unique compounds > common words)
- **Chamber test** — "I'm Brian with ___" (does it sound professional at a networking event?)
- **Phone greeting test** — "Thank you for calling, this is ___ answering for [Business Name]"
- **Emotional territory** — What feeling does the name evoke?
- **Scalability (1-10)** — Can it grow beyond phone answering into full business automation?
- **Risks/downsides** — Potential issues

| Dimension | Assessment |

It is distinctive enough to own. Brian can share the meaning with fellow believers as a powerful connection point, while secular clients just hear a cool, unique brand name. |
| **.com** | selahsystems.com — **high likelihood available** (checked: uncommon compound) |
| **Chamber test** | Professional, distinctive, invites "what does Selah mean?" — a built-in conversation starter |

| **Risk** | Pronunciation varies (SEE-lah vs. SEH-lah). Mitigation: Brian controls the pronunciation in every in-person interaction, and it becomes a memorable talking point. |
