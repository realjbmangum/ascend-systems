## 1. Market Context & Competitive Landscape

The AI phone answering market has exploded. As of early 2026, there are 30+ companies competing across three distinct tiers. Understanding where each sits — and where the gap is — drives every brand decision.

### Competitive Tier Map

| Tier | Examples | Monthly Cost | Setup Model | Relationship | Weakness |
|------|----------|-------------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **Budget DIY AI** | Dialzara, Allo, Trillet, Upfirst | $13–99/mo | Self-serve dashboard | None | Business owner must configure, train, and troubleshoot the AI themselves |
| **Mid-Tier AI Platforms** | Rosie, Goodcall, My AI Front Desk, NextPhone | $49–199/mo | Semi-guided setup | Email support | Better onboarding but still fundamentally self-serve; no local presence |
| **Premium Human Services** | Ruby, Smith.ai, AnswerHero | $293–1,640/mo | Managed by vendor | Remote account manager | Excellent quality but prohibitively expensive for most small businesses |
| **In-House Hire** | Receptionist employee | $3,500–5,500/mo | You manage everything | Direct but expensive | Calls in sick, quits, needs benefits, only works 40 hrs/week |

### Detailed Competitor Profiles

#### Smith.ai
- **Price:** AI-only from ~$97.50/mo; Live receptionist from $292.50/mo (30 calls)
- **Model:** Hybrid AI + human agents, per-call pricing (not per-minute)
- **Strengths:** 7,000+ integrations, custom voice, human backup, strong brand
- **Weakness:** Complex pricing tiers, no local relationship, enterprise feel
- **Positioning:** "24/7 AI Receptionist Backed by Live Agents"

#### Ruby
- **Price:** $309/mo (100 min) to $1,369/mo (500 min)
- **Model:** Live human receptionists, all US-based, per-minute billing
- **Strengths:** Exceptional quality, bilingual, 24/7, great reputation
- **Weakness:** Extremely expensive at scale; a plumber getting 300 calls/mo could pay $1,000+
- **Positioning:** "Live Virtual Receptionists: Never Miss a Call"

#### Dialzara
- **Price:** $29–349/mo
- **Model:** Pure AI, self-serve setup, 50+ voice options
- **Strengths:** Cheapest entry point, Zapier integrations, 7-day trial
- **Weakness:** DIY setup intimidates non-technical users; no human touch
- **Positioning:** "Best AI Receptionist Service"

#### Rosie
- **Price:** $49/mo (250 min) to $199/mo (1,000 min)
- **Model:** AI-only, self-serve, bilingual
- **Strengths:** Unlimited minutes on all plans, simple pricing, good for solopreneurs
- **Weakness:** No managed setup; business owners on their own after signup
- **Positioning:** "AI Call Answering Service | 24/7 Phone Support"

#### Goodcall
- **Price:** $59–199/mo per agent (unique caller-based billing)
- **Model:** AI-only with workflow builder, self-serve
- **Strengths:** Good CRM integration, lead capture, Google Voice support
- **Weakness:** Unique-caller pricing model is confusing; still self-serve
- **Positioning:** "AI Phone Agent and Virtual Receptionist"

#### Sameday AI
- **Price:** Flat monthly rate (custom quotes, home-services focused)
- **Model:** AI with deep ServiceTitan integration, managed onboarding
- **Strengths:** 92% booking rate, 15-min setup, built for contractors
- **Weakness:** Heavily tied to ServiceTitan; not general purpose
- **Positioning:** "AI Phone Answering for Home Services"

#### NextPhone
- **Price:** $199/mo flat rate, all-inclusive
- **Model:** AI-only, service-business focused
- **Strengths:** Simple pricing, no per-minute charges, 7-day trial
- **Weakness:** Still fundamentally a SaaS product, not a managed service
- **Positioning:** "AI Phone Answering for Service Businesses"

#### My AI Front Desk
- **Price:** $79–119/mo (annual billing)
- **Model:** AI-only, 100+ voice options, workflow builder
- **Strengths:** Good feature set, learns from every call
- **Weakness:** Self-serve setup, no local presence
- **Positioning:** "AI Receptionist | AI Phone Receptionists"

> [!success] The Gap in the Market
> **Every competitor is either:**
> - A self-serve SaaS platform (cheap but you're on your own), OR
> - A premium human service (great but $500+/month)
>
> **Nobody is offering:** A locally-operated, done-for-you managed AI service where a real person sets it up, trains it on your business, monitors it, and adjusts it — at a price between DIY bots and human receptionists.
>
> **That's the opening.**

## 2. Target Customer Profile

### Primary Persona: "Dave the Plumber"

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age** | 45–65 |
| **Business** | Local service business (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, dental, legal, contracting) |
| **Location** | Charlotte NC suburbs — Mint Hill, Matthews, Waxhaw, Monroe, Indian Trail |
| **Revenue** | $200K–$2M/year |
| **Employees** | 1–15 |
| **Tech comfort** | Uses a smartphone, QuickBooks, maybe a CRM. Does NOT want to learn new software. |
| **Pain point** | Misses calls while on jobs. Voicemail = lost customers. Wife/office manager is overwhelmed. |
| **Decision style** | Trusts handshakes and referrals. Skeptical of online-only vendors. Wants to see who he's paying. |
| **Budget sensitivity** | Will pay $199–499/mo if it clearly saves or makes him money. Won't pay $1,000+/mo. |

### What Dave Says (in his own words)
- "I'm losing jobs because I can't answer the phone when I'm under a house."
- "I tried one of those robot things online, couldn't figure it out."
- "My wife answers the phones but she's got her own job now."
- "I don't need fancy. I need someone to pick up the damn phone."

### Secondary Persona: "Sarah the Office Manager"
- Works at a dental practice, law firm, or multi-truck HVAC company
- Manages scheduling and incoming calls
- Overwhelmed, especially during peak hours
- Would love after-hours coverage so she can stop worrying at 5pm

## 7. Competitive Differentiation

### The Three Things That Make This Different

> [!warning] This is NOT just another AI answering app.

#### 1. Done-For-You Setup (vs. DIY Platforms)

| DIY Platforms (Dialzara, Goodcall, Rosie) | CallSteady |
|-------------------------------------------|------------|
| You sign up online | Brian meets you at your office or at coffee |
| You configure your own AI agent | Brian configures it based on your actual call patterns |
| You write your own scripts | Brian writes scripts that sound like YOUR business |
| You troubleshoot when it breaks | Brian monitors and fixes before you notice |
| You're a user in a SaaS dashboard | You're a client with a dedicated partner |

**Why this matters:** A plumber doesn't want to log into a dashboard and tweak AI prompts. He wants to hand someone his business card and say "make my phones work" — and have it happen.

#### 2. Local Presence (vs. Remote-Only Services)

| Remote Services (Ruby, Smith.ai, all SaaS) | CallSteady |
|---------------------------------------------|------------|
| Sales come from Google Ads and cold email | Sales come from Chamber meetings and referrals |
| Support is a ticket queue | Support is a phone call to Brian |
| They don't know your market | Brian knows Mint Hill, Matthews, and Monroe |
| They serve 10,000 clients remotely | Brian serves 50 clients personally |
| Trust is earned through reviews | Trust is earned through a handshake |

**Why this matters:** Local service businesses trust local people. A Mint Hill dentist would rather pay $299/mo to a guy he met at the Chamber than $29/mo to a website he found on Google.

#### 3. AI-Powered Pricing (vs. Human Receptionist Costs)

| Human Services (Ruby, Smith.ai live) | CallSteady |
|--------------------------------------|------------|
| $309–1,640/mo for limited minutes | $199–499/mo flat rate |
| Per-minute billing creates anxiety | Predictable monthly cost |
| Overages are expensive | No surprise charges |
| Quality degrades with volume | AI handles any volume consistently |
| Agents call in sick, need training | AI is available 24/7/365 |

**Why this matters:** Ruby is great, but $705/mo for their most popular plan is too much for a solo plumber. CallSteady delivers 80% of Ruby's quality at 40% of the cost, with zero per-minute anxiety.

## 8. Brand Voice & Personality

### Personality Traits

The brand has five core personality traits, in order of importance:

| Trait | What It Means | How It Shows Up |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Dependable** | The #1 trait. This brand shows up every time, no excuses. | Messaging always leads with reliability. Guarantees. Consistency. |
| **Straightforward** | No jargon, no buzzwords, no corporate speak. Says what it means. | Short sentences. Plain English. Specific claims over vague promises. |
| **Local** | Feels like a neighbor, not a corporation. Knows your community. | References to Charlotte area, small business realities, local events. |
| **Capable** | Knows its stuff. Confident without being arrogant. | Demonstrates expertise through specifics, not through credentials. |
| **Approachable** | Easy to talk to. Not intimidating. Warm but professional. | Conversational tone. First-person. Avoids formality for formality's sake. |

### Voice Guidelines

#### How CallSteady Talks

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Website hero** | Confident, clear, benefit-driven | "Every call answered. Every customer helped. You never lift a finger." |
| **Website body** | Conversational, informative, specific | "When you're on a job site and a customer calls, CallSteady picks up, gets their info, and books the appointment. You get a text with the details." |
| **Email outreach** | Warm, personal, local | "Hey Dave — I'm Brian, I run CallSteady here in Mint Hill. I help local businesses stop missing phone calls. Mind if I show you how it works?" |
| **Chamber pitch** | Casual-professional, concise | "I set up AI receptionists for local businesses. My clients never miss a call. It costs less than you'd think." |
| **Sales follow-up** | Helpful, no-pressure | "Just checking in — still interested in getting your phones covered? Happy to answer any questions." |
| **Support/service** | Responsive, ownership-taking | "I see the issue. I'm adjusting your call scripts now — should be fixed within the hour." |

#### Do's and Don'ts

| DO Say | DON'T Say |
|--------|-----------|
| "Your phones are covered." | "Leverage our AI-powered communication solution." |
| "We answer your calls." | "Our proprietary NLP engine processes inbound communications." |
| "I'll set it up for you." | "Onboard in minutes with our self-serve dashboard." |
| "You get a text with the details." | "Real-time push notifications via our omnichannel platform." |
| "It costs $299 a month. That's it." | "Flexible pricing tiers to optimize your ROI." |
| "I'm based right here in Mint Hill." | "Our distributed team serves clients globally." |
| "Let me show you how it sounds." | "Schedule a demo to experience our synergistic solution." |
| "Your customers won't know it's AI." | "Powered by cutting-edge generative artificial intelligence." |

#### What CallSteady Would NEVER Say

- "Disrupting the phone answering industry" — We're not disrupting. We're serving.
- "AI-first" or "AI-native" — Our customers don't care about AI. They care about answered calls.
- "Scale your operations" — Our customers aren't scaling. They're trying to keep up.
- "Optimize your funnel" — Nobody in our audience has a funnel. They have a phone.
- "Leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem," "paradigm" — Banned vocabulary.
- Any claim about being "#1" or "the best" — Let the service speak for itself.

### Writing Rules

1. **Lead with the benefit, not the feature.** Not "AI-powered call handling" but "Every call answered, 24/7."
2. **Use numbers when possible.** Not "affordable" but "$299/month, flat."
3. **Write like you talk.** Read it out loud. If it sounds weird, rewrite it.
4. **One idea per sentence.** Short sentences build trust. Long ones lose people.
5. **Name the pain before the solution.** "Missing calls costs you money. CallSteady fixes that."

## 10. Brand Architecture

### Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         LIGHTHOUSE 27 LLC               │
│         (Parent Company)                │
│         - Legal entity                  │
│         - Holds all brands              │
│         - Invisible to customers        │
│                                         │
│    ┌─────────────┐  ┌──────────────┐    │
│    │  CALLSTEADY  │  │ Future Brand │    │
│    │  (Product)   │  │   (TBD)     │    │
│    │              │  │              │    │
│    │  AI Phone    │  │  Next        │    │
│    │  Answering   │  │  Product     │    │
│    └─────────────┘  └──────────────┘    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### How Each Entity Appears

| Context | What Appears | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Website** | CallSteady brand only | "CallSteady" in header, logo, all copy |
| **Website footer** | Small legal attribution | "CallSteady is a service of Lighthouse 27 LLC" |
| **Business cards** | CallSteady brand, Brian's name | "Brian Mangum / CallSteady / callsteady.com" |
| **Chamber meeting** | CallSteady only | "I'm Brian with CallSteady" |
| **Contracts** | Lighthouse 27 LLC as legal entity | "This agreement is between Lighthouse 27 LLC d/b/a CallSteady and..." |
| **Invoices** | Lighthouse 27 LLC with CallSteady | Invoice from Lighthouse 27 LLC, branded as CallSteady |
| **Bank account** | Lighthouse 27 LLC | Business checking under parent entity |
| **LLC filing** | Lighthouse 27 LLC | May file DBA for "CallSteady" in NC |

### Key Decisions

1. **CallSteady is the customer-facing brand.** Lighthouse 27 never appears in marketing.
2. **Brian is the face.** The brand is personal. "Brian from CallSteady" is the pitch, not "CallSteady, an AI company."
3. **Parent company enables future brands.** If Brian launches a second product (e.g., AI texting for restaurants), it gets its own brand under Lighthouse 27.
4. **File a DBA.** Register "CallSteady" as a Doing Business As name under Lighthouse 27 LLC in North Carolina. This is simple, inexpensive, and provides legal clarity.
